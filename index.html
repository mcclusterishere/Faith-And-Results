The work

Four pillars. One mission.

FREEDOM was organized as a think-tank collaborative of senior pastors, grassroots leaders, and business and industry — working where faith and economics meet.

01

Economic Development

Financial empowerment with the institutions that move money — from foreclosure prevention with the FDIC and Federal Home Loan Banks to community lending partnerships.

See how this works

Foreclosure prevention and intervention programs built with the FDIC, HUD, and the Federal Home Loan Banks of Atlanta, Boston, and San Francisco — plus state and municipal housing finance authorities — bringing bank-grade tools to church-anchored communities.

02

Education & Training

Workforce and business training designed and delivered nationally — including training for NeighborWorks America, the country's largest HUD housing intermediary.

See how this works

The Connecticut Faith Jobs Collaborative designs workforce programs — race and gender neutral by design — that move residents into building-trade employment and training, through the congregations they already trust.

03

Housing

Homes saved and homes built: foreclosure intervention, housing counseling, and development with state and municipal housing finance authorities.

See how this works

The FREEDOM–Faith CDC collaborative develops faith-based housing — congregations turning land and trust into homes, with the finance authorities at the table from day one.

04

Sustainability

Building congregations and community organizations that outlast any single grant — capacity, governance, and revenue that renews itself.

See how this works

Proof of concept at home: under Rev. McCluster, Shiloh Baptist became the first African-American church in Connecticut to reach energy self-sufficiency through solar.

About

Led from the pulpit and the boardroom.

“Initiate and nurture renewal and restoration for our communities — in faith, with results.”

Rev. Carl McCluster

Senior Pastor of Shiloh Baptist Church in Bridgeport, Connecticut — a pulpit he has held for more than two decades — and Managing Director of FREEDOM. Founder of the Faith Community Development Corporation, the faith-based CDC chartered by Shiloh, and former chairperson of Bridgeport's South End Neighborhood Revitalization Zone.

The fuller story

Trained in both economies — a Bachelor of Arts in Political Economy from Colorado College and a Master of Divinity from the Samuel DeWitt Proctor School of Theology at Virginia Union University. Under his leadership, Shiloh became the first African-American church in Connecticut to reach energy self-sufficiency through solar — sustainability preached and practiced.

The record

Consulting for the FDIC, HUD, and the Federal Home Loan Banks of Atlanta, Boston, and San Francisco. Trainer for NeighborWorks America. Partnerships with state and municipal housing finance authorities and financial institutions across the country. (Impact numbers publish here as the client supplies them — people trained, homes saved, dollars moved.)

The roots

FREEDOM began as a think-tank collaborative of senior pastors, grassroots leaders, and business and industry across Texas, Georgia, Maryland and D.C., California, Colorado, Connecticut, Louisiana, and New York — and grew into a national network delivering programs in seventeen American cities.
