# Church OS
### One machine, in your building, that runs the back office so you can do the ministry.

---

## The problem

A church runs on records. Who is a member, who has not been seen in a month, who
gave what and when, who volunteered, what is on the calendar, who needs a call.
Today that lives in a dozen places: a spreadsheet, a giving app, a mailing list,
a filing cabinet, and the pastor's memory. Every one of those is a monthly bill,
a separate login, and a copy of your congregation's most private information
sitting on some company's server far from the building.

Pastors did not answer a call to do data entry. Staff burn hours on admin that
should go to people.

## The offer

**Church OS is one machine that lives in your building and runs the back office.**

It keeps every record in one place. It drafts the newsletter, reconciles the
giving, tracks attendance, runs the calendar and the text blasts, and quietly
tells the pastor "these twelve families have not been here in a month" so he can
reach out. It answers questions in plain language: *"How did giving compare to
last year?"* *"Who volunteered for the food drive?"* *"Draft a note to the new
visitors from Sunday."*

It assists the pastor and the staff. It does not replace them, and it never
touches the spiritual heart of the church. It gives you your week back.

## The promise that matters most

**Your data never leaves the building.**

Church OS runs on your own machine, on your own premises, owned by you. Your
members' records, your giving history, your pastoral-care notes: none of it goes
to the cloud, none of it is sold, none of it is mined. In a moment when
congregations rightly distrust Big Tech with their people, this is the whole
point. The records belong to the church, and they stay in the church.

Money is always in human hands. The system tracks, reconciles, and drafts the
report, but a person approves every transaction and everything is logged for the
audit. Sensitive pastoral notes are visible only to those who should see them.

## Why Shiloh first

Shiloh Baptist Church and the Faith Community Development Corporation are the
proving ground. We run the real operation on it, we measure what it saves in
hours and in families re-engaged, and Shiloh becomes the model every other
church can see working before they say yes.

## The model

1. Prove it at Shiloh.
2. Package it as one standard machine with setup and support.
3. Install it into churches across the Faith CDC network and beyond.

Every install is a church that owns its own operation instead of renting it from
five different companies. It is the FREEDOM mission in a box: build capacity,
keep it local, put the tools in the community's own hands.
